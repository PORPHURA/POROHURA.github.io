Research Experience

May, 2017 - Now

Non-line-of-sight (NLOS) imaging by improved algorithm

The recovery of objects obscured by scattering is an important goal in imaging. In this project, our core algorithm of the reconstruction is a probability-calculation method based on the Bayesian statistics using the information of each sampling point on the wall, including both the illuminance and the flight time. I have a first-author paper on this topic in contribution.

June 2018 - Now

SNR-adaptive OCT angiography

In this work, based on a multi-variate time series model, we derived an asymptotic linear relation of between inverse SNR and decorrelation (ID). Accordingly, with the population distribution of static and noise voxels being explicitly calculated in the ID space, a classifier is developed by removing static and noise voxels at all SNR, to generate a SNR-adaptive OCTA, i.e., ID-OCTA.

July 2018 - Aug. 2018

Generation of tunable Bessel beam

This project aims to investigate methods of engineering the axial light intensity distribution of Bessel beam, and explore its application in other fields. The basic thought is utilising SLM to approximate expected field on focal plane because of the Fourier transformation associating the complex field on SLM and the field on the back focal plane. This work is presented as a session poster in Westlake Photonics Symposium.
